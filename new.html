The Founder

Born and raised in Milan, Antilla is an architect and designer. After obtaining a Master’s degree with honors in Sustainable Architecture from Politecnico di Milano, she started her career at Patricia Urquiola Studio in Milan. Driven by a deep-rooted passion for travelling and for luxury design, she then relocated to Dubai to work for HBA/Hirsch Bedner Associates, the world’s leading hospitality interior design firm. Antilla has designed some of the world’s most prestigious hotels for operators such as Four Seasons, Langham, St. Regis and was awarded HBA Dubai Designer of The Year in 2018. Fascinated by learning from new challenges and crossing design and cultural boundaries she then moved to Saudi Arabia to work for the Crown Prince Private Affairs where she managed the design of His Royal Highness private palaces. Building on these remarkable experiences, Antilla decided to start developing her own designs and focusing on creating intriguing and refined rugs for today’s lifestyle. Her experimental approach celebrates the juxtaposition between sophistication and comfort, multi-layered and contemporary, poetry and geometry, avoiding style constraints and thinking out of the box. Nourished by tireless attention to detail and engaging color palettes, vibrant yet welcoming, her unconventional creations not only capture the eye but foster an emotive connection.

An Ethical World

With sustainability and ethics being at the heart of everything we do, we partner with two leading organizations in environment-conscious practices and social responsibility: Label STEP and GoodWeave. With these collaborations, we strive towards weaving a world where virtuous production is the norm and each product proudly bears the emblem of mindful craftsmanship. Label STEP focuses on certifying sustainable practices in textile production: it assesses factors such as chemical management, environmental performance, social responsibility, and quality management. On the other hand, our collaboration with GoodWeave reinforces our unwavering stance against child labor in the rug industry, supporting the well-being of workers and empowering communities. By partnering with Label STEP and GoodWeave, we actively contribute to a brighter, more equitable world, one rug at a time.

Najdi architecture, from Saudi Arabia's Najd region, boasts a mesmerizing style characterized by the use of triangular shapes in buildings, decorative elements and textiles. We strive to create meaningful handmade pieces where design blends with art and craftmanship using passion, creativity and experience.
The ancient art of rug making is a practiced by many, yet understood by a precious few. Distant from mass production we aim to offer meticulously crafted rugs to discerning individuals who appreciate the finer things in life, seek and enjoy a high end product, where a long production time encapsulates the timeless heritage of rich culture.
We are committed not only to preserving traditional craftsmanship but also supporting the artisans who create these rugs. Our artisans come from communities where rug-making has been a part of their cultural heritage for generations, and their skills are passed down from one generation to the next.
More than a rug, every piece is a powerful expression of the limitless imagination and uniqueness of the human spirit, a tale of dedication and artistry that speak of the artisan’s pursuit of perfection, and an embodiment of dreams woven into reality.

Manufacturing Techniques /

Hand Tufting /

Hand-tufting is a remarkable textile weaving technique that requires the skillful hands of artisans to manually insert yarn tufts onto a cotton backing using a tool called a tufting gun. The cotton backing acts as a canvas that features the rug’s traced design, and by shooting yarn through the tufting gun into the fabric frame, these artisans bring the design to life. Meticulously cut and shaved with specialized scissors, the yarn transforms into captivating curved patterns and piles of varying heights and densities showcasing the weaver’s artistic prowess. Hand tufting is an art form without limits, where the realm of materials and design possibilities expands infinitely. From a rich selection of materials to an endless array of designs, shapes, and colors, the creative potential knows no bounds, offering a limitless playground for artistic exploration. The end result of the hand tufting process is a one-of-a-kind, handcrafted rug. Each rug bears the mark of the artisan’s expertise and creativity, exhibiting a luxurious feel, intricate design, and exceptional visual appeal.

Color Dyeing /

In the enchanting world of hand-tufted rugs, the journey begins with a vibrant splash of color. Before the artistry unfolds, the yarns undergo a meticulous dyeing process, ensuring each hue is a true masterpiece in its own right. This process gives the first level of uniqueness to each rug because no two dye baths are ever the same. Artisans skillfully wind the yarn onto a wheel-like frame, and it is then immersed into boiling cauldrons of dye, where its fibers absorb the chosen colors. Basking in the sun, the yarns dry with renewed vibrancy and then the weavers spin them into balls. To guide their skilled hands, a color card is also crafted, each color is assigned a number, harmonizing the palette with the weaving pattern.

Graph Creation /

The creation of a graph serves as a blueprint, guiding artisans on their journey to bring intricate designs to life. By transferring the design onto specialized graph paper or digital software, each stitch is granted meticulous precision and flawless alignment. With its grid-like structure, each square becomes a sacred space, cradling a specific tuft and reverencing the art of attention to detail. Crafting this graph demands unwavering dedication and a profound comprehension of the design’s intricacies, showcasing the artisans’ artistry and skill. It is a testament to the artisans’ mastery, leading them through the transformative process of transforming a blank canvas into a captivating hand-tufted rug.

Tufting the design /

In the art of hand tufting, master artisans wield a hand-held tool of artistic precision known as the tufting gun. With an unwavering eye for detail, the artisans select the yarn colors and load the gun with a spool of creative potential. The tufting gun becomes an extension of their skilled hands ready to weave magic into the fabric: the artisans skillfully maneuver the tufting gun along the previously crafted graph, crafting loops and gradually building up the desired pattern.

CARVING, BACKING AND FINISHING /

As the tufting process comes to an end, the rug is finally freed from its frame. At this stage, the loops of yarn on the front side remain intact, creating a looped pile surface. Now this is where the true artistry of carving comes into play, as craftsmen work their magic by trimming or cutting specific areas to add depth and dimension. The rug is then carefully backed with a secondary material ensuring the yarn tufts stay firmly in place while also adding structural support. Finally, the hand-tufted rug is brought to life with the final touches: every tuft, every thread, and every contour is examined to ensure the rug is nothing less than a masterpiece. This is the culmination of a process that has been a labor of love from start to finish. Every step, from the tufting to the carving and backing, has been carefully executed to create a rug that is not only beautiful but also tells a story.

Materials /

Wools /

Sheep’s wool, a timeless gem in the world of rug-making, has earned its esteemed reputation for unmatched durability and innate softness. With yarns that can be knotted, woven, or tufted, it allows for versatile production. At the heart of our craft lies the pursuit of excellence, which is why we choose the extraordinary quality of New Zealand wool. This coarse yet robust wool ensures longevity, while its pristine whiteness enhances the dyeing process, resulting in vibrant hues. As a renewable and eco-friendly fiber, wool aligns perfectly with our commitment to sustainability. And to add even more intrigue, we also work with angora, cashmere, alpaca, and mohair to craft unique products with varied textures.

Bamboo Silk /

Bamboo silk is a natural fiber that is extracted from the bamboo plant. The rapid growth of this plant makes it a renewable resource and avoids deforestation. The silk obtained from processing bamboo fiber has a luxurious texture, softness, and sheen similar to natural silk, representing a more sustainable alternative. Moreover, its natural properties make it very resistant, hypoallergenic, antibacterial, breathable, protective from UV rays and 100% biodegradable.

Recycled Plastic /

As part of our commitment to reducing plastic waste and carbon emissions, we proudly use recycled PET yarns while also raising awareness of the alarming forecast that by 2050, there will be more plastic than fish in the ocean. This ecological yarn is created exclusively from post-consumer PET plastic bottles that have been converted into fibers using an innovative process. These fibers are then spun into yarn, which maintains an extraordinary level of softness while also possessing uncompromised qualities such as water repellency, flame retardancy, UV resistance, and stain and mildew resistance. Additionally, recycled PET yarn is infinitely recyclable without losing its quality.
