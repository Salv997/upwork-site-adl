Autumn, being a time of transition, represents a period of change in which the natural world readies itself, prompting individuals to meditate on the transience of life and the beauty of impermanence.

ALMADRA /

The Almadraba fishing technique, a complex and ancient netting system used off the Atlantic coast of Spain to catch prized blue-fin tuna, has a rich cultural heritage that dates back to the Phoenicians, Romans, and Moors. Almadra rug pays tribute to it with its intricate design of interwoven lines and geometric shapes, serving as a celebration of the enduring tradition and artistry of fishing, as well as a homage to Spain's cultural heritage.

DIRIYAH /

Najdi architecture, from Saudi Arabia's Najd region, boasts a mesmerizing style characterized by the use of triangular shapes in buildings, decorative elements and textiles. These triangles are arranged in a hypnotic and repetitive pattern to symbolize stability, strength, and balance. The Diriyah rug is a captivating tribute to Najdi motifs, interlaced with fading elements that recall the everlasting inspiration of history. Bold shapes bind it all together, celebrating the timeless allure of blending old and new.
